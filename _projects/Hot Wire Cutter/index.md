## Overview
Before I could cut the foam cores for my composite wing panel, I needed a way to shape them precisely and repeatably. Off-the-shelf foam cutters didn't fit my budget or my design needs, so I built my own hot wire cutter, a frame-mounted, temperature-controlled nichrome wire rig capable of slicing clean airfoil profiles out of XPS foam. Using only wood panels left over from my balcony rebuild, some five-dollar nichrome wire bought off Amazon, and a ten-year-old PC power supply, I was able to build a fully functional cutter for under $10 in new materials.

This project became less about the cut itself and more about solving a problem and designing and building a tool: thinking through frame rigidity, wire tension, and safe, controllable heat output.

## The Build

### Mechanical Rig
The frame is the backbone of the whole system. It needs to hold the nichrome wire taut and perfectly straight under heat and tension, without warping or drifting mid-cut.

{% include image-gallery.html images="IMG_4831.HEIC" height="400" %}
 
### Wiring and Power
Getting consistent, controllable heat meant carefully wiring the nichrome wire to a power source, with attention to current draw and resistance so the wire stays hot enough to melt foam cleanly without snapping. I settled on a 12V supply, giving enough current through the nichrome wire to melt foam cleanly without risking a burnout.
